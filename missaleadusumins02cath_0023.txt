In regard to the List of York Service Books, it may be worth
while adding a short account of a unique volume, “Expositio
Hymnorum et Sequentiarum,'' published by Wansfort at York,
1507. The volume is in possession of Mr. Kerslake, of Bristol‚

who very kindly placed it in my hands. It is not an exposition
of hymns and sequences of the York Use, though doubtless
intended to be used in the Diocese, but identical with that accord-
ing to the Use of Sarum. The words “Secundum usum Sarum’⁷
have been omitted, and new title-pageS, colophons‚ and intro-
ductory letters inserted. The volume does not appear to have
been intended in any way for liturgical use, but as a school-
book, as will be seen from the introductory letters, which, with
the titles and colophons, Ῑ subjoin. I have added part of the
Exposition of the Sequence for St. Catharine, which will give
a clear idea of the character of the Exposition.
